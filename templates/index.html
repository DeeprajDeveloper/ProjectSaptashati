What's Devi Saptashati (a.k.a Chandi Path) ?
Devi Saptashati, also known as the Durga Saptashati or Chandi Path, is a highly revered Hindu scripture, consisting of 700 verses (Sapta (सप्त्) meaning 7 and Shata (शत्) meaning 100, hence the name "Saptashati"). It forms a crucial part of the Markandeya Purana and is dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga in her various forms. The text narrates the story of the cosmic struggle between the divine and demonic forces, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil, and is a spiritual guide for invoking the grace and blessings of the Divine Mother.
Recitation and Worship

The Durga Saptashati is often recited during Navaratri, especially in its Chandi Path (चण्डी पाठः) form. The devotees believe that chanting these verses with faith and dedication invokes the protective and nurturing aspects of the Divine Mother. The reading of the entire 700 verses, however, is a profound spiritual discipline and is often performed as part of a larger worship ritual.
